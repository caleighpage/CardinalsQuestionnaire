Additionally, using these same data resources, develop a model trained on 2017-2020 seasons where the goal is to classify all available 2021 offensive SF plays as either “pass” or “run” based on the following variables: down, distance, time remaining, field position, score differential, and any other variables you find to be significant. In your submission, please include a csv of the 2021 predicted plays, a write-up of your method and results, and all the code used to obtain these predictions, ensuring reproducibility.

I began by doing some explanatory data analysis of the predictor variables before modeling. The visualizations below show the density distributions of the predictors outlined in the question.

I then creating an xgboost classification model with the variables included in the questions. After looking looking at the results of the model, I suspected that other variables could have an influence on whether an offensive play is a pass or run. I chose to add quarter, time remaining in the half, drive, whether or not a team is in a goal down situation, and the number of timeouts the offensive team has left. I added quarter and time remaining in half to account for late in the halves when teams tend to throw more passes if they are behind or tend to run more in take time off the clock if they are winning. I included whether or not a team is in a goal down situation to account for tendencies in key situations. Finally, I included the number of timeouts a the offense has left to account for how they might handle play calling late in the game depending on the score differential. Once these additional predictor variables were included, I trained an xgboost and random forest model using cross validation to tune the parameters. I then ran a logistic regression model on the training data. I used stacks() in the tidymodels package as an ensemble step to combine the three models in hopes of decreasing the number of errors and improve prediction accuracy. The final stacks model predicted the play type all SF offensive plays with an accuracy of 68%.
